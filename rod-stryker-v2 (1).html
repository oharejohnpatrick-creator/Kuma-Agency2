Common Considerations

Depth is not determined by physical proximity but by structure, accountability, and content rigor. This program employs a sequential, assessed model that often creates deeper integration than passive attendance at in-person trainings. Daily practice logs, written reflections, and teaching submissions ensure the material is embodied, not just observed. The format also allows for 12 months of assimilation rather than 30 days of overwhelm.

Most affordable trainings offer video libraries without assessment, lineage, or pedagogical structure. This program provides: (1) A cumulative curriculum where each module builds systematically, (2) Personal feedback on teaching practicums and written work, (3) Direct transmission from a teacher with 40+ years of Himalayan lineage, and (4) Yoga Alliance 500-HR eligibility. The value lies in the outcome: true teaching competency.

The program is designed to be completed over 12 months, requiring approximately 8-10 hours weekly. This includes lecture viewing, reading, personal practice, and written work. It is structured to accommodate working teachers—you can view lectures asynchronously, though live Q&A sessions are offered monthly.

Upon completion, you receive a 300-hour certificate from ParaYoga, qualifying you for the 500-hour RYT designation through Yoga Alliance (assuming you hold a 200-hour RYT). Rod Stryker's involvement in establishing Yoga Alliance standards ensures this certification is recognized globally.
